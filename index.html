About Me

My name is John Thomas Bicket, most people call me JT, and I am a 17 year old high school student from Charlotte, North Carolina. I am an avid martial artist and amateur web programmer, among other things... In my free time I enjoy working out, having meaningful conversations, and hanging with friends. I have a pretty broad interest in music, and I like action/adventure movies. During the summer I enjoy wakeboarding, cliff diving, and driving with the convertible top down. In the winter I like snowboarding, building log fires, and wishing it was summer!

I am currently a junior in high school, and I plan on attending college to major in Business. I've always had an interest in business, leadership, and technology - something that might sound a little mature for my age, but ehh... at least I know what I want to do. My dream job would be working as a CEO or CIO (head of technology) for a company. I am an active part of my church's youth group and volunteer at things like JoyProm. Welcome to my website!

Martial Arts

I have been actively training in Martial Arts since 2008. In 2013 I earned my 1st Degree Blackbelt. In 2015 I earned my 2nd Degree Blackbelt, my current rank. Since May of 2014 I have been apart of the teaching staff at my martial arts school. Being able to coach others, teach classes, and train as my high-school job is such an awesome experience. I get to do something I enjoy, stay in shape, teach others, train with friends, and build skills I will use later in life - I am humbly grateful for the opportunity to work there. The Boxing and Judo aspect of training is what I enjoy most, something that I plan on continuing in college.

Martial Arts has opened up a wide range of opportuntiies for me. One of these is a trip I'm taking this summer to Los Angeles, California. For a week me and some of the other blackbelts at our school will be training at the International Martial Arts and Boxing Academy, the Tempest Freerunning Academy, and a few others. I can't wait for this trip and I'm sure it will be a highlight of my summer this year!

Web Design

My Website

How It All Began

I started building www.jtbicket.com in January 2016. It originally began as a project for my web design class at school, but it quickly grew into a full fledged programming project. As I studied more and more about web development I decided to launch my own personal website where I could practice all of the things I was learning.

The Reason

There are a couple of reasons for why I decided to create this website:

  • A good online presence. Technology is continuing to improve and the web is becoming biggger and biger every day. It is more and more common for people to Google one another these days and the results that come up can create a positive or negative impression on their opinion of you before they even get to know you. Just as your want to have a good first impression when you meet someone, I want to have a good online presence when people look me up. Being able to have a positive online presence is good for future jobs, internships, and collages as I'm able to give them insight into who I am without them having the opportunity to meet me.

  • A unique website. Instagram, Twitter, Facebook, and other social media sites always have a place in your about section to put a URL link to a website. I've always wanted to be able to put something there as people don't normally use that space. Now...I can XD. This is less of a reason to why I made this site and more of just a kinda of humorous benefit.

  • A place to practice. I created www.jtbicket.com so I could practice many of the new things that I was learning in Web Development. I wanted to continue trying new things and improving on what I had created. My own website also allows me to show my latest work in web design and allows for me try out new ideas and inspirations.

The Process

From inception to launch this website took over a month to develop, write, and publish. I goal is to always to give off this sense of orginally in my work, as it is much more difficult (and fulfilling) when you actually code a web project on your own as opposed to just using a website creator. I don't do this to show off, but rather to display the progress I'm making in learning web programming. I'm proud of what I've accomplished so far, but I understand that there is still much for me to learn. My personal website will improve over time as I gain more and more experience - my goal is to always be working on new ideas for my site and other projects.
